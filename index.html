UNDERSTANDING FORTUNE

While there are many ways to measure the success of a company, the Fortune 500 rankings are made purely based off of annual revenue, making it possible for companies who have negative annual profits to still be in the ranking.

OVERSPENDING ?

The efficiency ratio* is a measure of the companies spendings. Companies usually aim for the sweet-spot of 60-80% but more often than not, fail.


The lower the efficiency ratio, the higher a company's profits. Curious to see if your favourite company succeeds?


*efficiency ratio = expenses / revenue

If the company's efficiency ratio is above 80%, it is likely that they are overspending.

FUTURE IS FEMALE?

Only 1 in 11 CEOs are women

While the number of female CEOs in the Fortune 500 has increased from 10 to 12, the lack of equality in leadership could reflect a more serious issue in corporate America. In future rankings, this number should steadily increase to reflect the proportion of women in the workplace.
